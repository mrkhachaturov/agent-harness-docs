# Profiles

Claim a handle to get a shareable public profile at `cursor.com/@yourhandle`.

## What is a public profile?

Your public profile is a single page that shows your Cursor identity. Once you claim a handle, anyone with the link can view it at `cursor.com/@yourhandle`.

A profile shows:

- Your name and profile picture from your Cursor account
- The date you joined Cursor
- Up to four links you add to anywhere you want
- Usage data that shows your Cursor activity over time

## How do I claim my handle?

1. Go to [cursor.com/profile](https://cursor.com/profile) and sign in
2. Enter the handle you want and confirm

Your handle can use lowercase letters, numbers, and single hyphens, and must be at least three characters long. Handles are unique and first come, first served. Some handles are reserved and can't be claimed.

Choose carefully. You can't change your handle after you claim it. When you claim a handle, your profile's default visibility depends on your account: if you're not on a team, your profile is public by default; if you're on a team, it's visible to your teammates by default.

Cursor may change, remove, or reclaim any handle at its discretion. For example, we may do this if a handle matches a Cursor product or feature name, infringes someone's rights, impersonates another person or organization, violates our policies, or is used for abuse or other harmful activity. If we reclaim yours, we'll notify you when practical, and you will then have the opportunity to claim a new one.

## How do I add my links?

Go to [cursor.com/profile](https://cursor.com/profile) and add up to four links pointing anywhere you like, such as your X, GitHub, LinkedIn, or personal site. Links show on your public profile only when the public toggle is on.

Your name and profile picture come from your Cursor account, so update those in your account settings. Edits appear on your live profile the next time it loads.

## How do I make my profile public or private?

Use the **Public profile** toggle in your profile settings.

- **On**: anyone can view your profile at `cursor.com/@yourhandle`
- **Off**: your profile is no longer public. If you're on a team, your teammates can still view it while signed in. If you're not on a team, only you can see it while signed in as the owner.

You keep your handle either way. Turning the toggle off removes the page from the public web without releasing your handle.

## Who can see my profile?

There are three visibility levels:

- **Public**: anyone with the link can view your profile, including people who aren't signed in to Cursor.
- **Team**: only your teammates can view your profile, and only while signed in to Cursor. It isn't served on the public web. This is the default for team members.
- **Private**: only you can see your profile while signed in as the owner. This is what the toggle's off state means if you're not on a team.

Switching away from public stops your profile from being served publicly.

## How do team admins control profile visibility?

If you're on a team, your admin sets a **Profile visibility** policy in the team settings that caps how visible team members' profiles can be:

- **Private**: profiles are visible only to the member.
- **Team** (default): profiles are visible to the member's teammates, but not on the public web.
- **Public**: members can choose to make their profile public on cursor.com.

You can always claim a handle and set up your profile; the policy only limits who can see it. If you're on more than one team, the most restrictive policy applies.

## What usage data shows on my profile?

Your profile shows usage data that reflects your Cursor activity over time. This updates as you use Cursor.

## What happens to my profile if I delete my account?

Deleting your Cursor account also deletes your public profile and frees your handle. See [Delete your account](https://cursor.com/help/account-and-billing/delete-account.md) for the full process.
